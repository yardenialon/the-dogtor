Seeing blood in your dog's poop is alarming — and while some causes are minor, one in particular can turn dangerous fast: **Acute Haemorrhagic Diarrhoea Syndrome (AHDS)**, the condition vets used to call **HGE**. Knowing what the blood looks like, and which signs mean "go now," can be the difference that saves your dog.

## First, what kind of blood is it?

There are two patterns, and they point to different places in the gut:

- **Bright red blood (haematochezia)** — fresh blood from the **lower** bowel or rectum. Often seen as streaks on firm stool or mixed into diarrhoea.
- **Dark, black, tarry stool (melena)** — digested blood from **higher up** in the stomach or small intestine.

A **small streak** on an otherwise normal stool, in a **bright, well dog**, is often minor. **Large amounts** of blood — or bloody diarrhoea — are a different story.

## AHDS (HGE): the one to know

**AHDS** is a sudden, severe illness where the gut lining leaks fluid and blood. The classic picture is:

- **Profuse bloody diarrhoea** that often looks like **raspberry jam**
- Usually **vomiting** as well
- Rapid onset — a dog can go from normal to very sick in **hours**

The real danger isn't just the blood — it's the **massive fluid loss**, which can cause **dehydration and shock** quickly, especially in **small and toy breeds**. That's why AHDS is treated as an **emergency**.

## Red flags: go to a vet now

Treat blood in the stool as an **emergency** if you see any of these:

- A **large amount** of blood, or **bloody / raspberry-jam diarrhoea**
- **Repeated vomiting**
- A **bloated, tense or painful belly**
- **Pale gums, weakness, or collapse**
- Your dog is a **puppy, small/toy breed, or senior**, or has other illnesses

> A puppy with bloody diarrhoea and vomiting also needs urgent testing for **parvovirus**, a serious and highly contagious disease.

## When it can (probably) wait

A **single small streak** of bright red blood on a formed stool, in a dog that is **bright, eating and playful**, is often a minor irritation. Even then, **call your vet** for advice — they may want a same-day or next-day check and a stool sample to rule out parasites.

## How AHDS is treated

There's no home remedy for AHDS. Vets provide **aggressive supportive care**:

- **Intravenous fluids** to reverse dehydration and shock — the cornerstone of treatment
- **Anti-nausea and gut-protective** medication
- **Monitoring** of hydration and blood values
- **Antibiotics only if sepsis is suspected** — not routinely

With **prompt** treatment, most dogs recover well. The danger comes from **waiting**.

## The bottom line

A tiny streak of blood in a happy dog is often minor — but sudden, heavy, raspberry-jam bloody diarrhoea, or any dog that's weak, vomiting or collapsing, is an emergency that can escalate within hours.
