Your dog is sneezing, has a runny nose, or has started a honking cough — and the first question every owner asks is: *is this just a cold, or something like kennel cough?* Dogs **don't catch human colds**, but they do get their own respiratory infections, and knowing what to watch for tells you whether to wait it out or call the vet.

## Cold vs. kennel cough: the quick read

| Mild upper-airway irritation | Kennel cough (canine respiratory infection) |
| --- | --- |
| Occasional sneeze, **clear** runny nose | Loud, **hacking cough**, often ends in a gag/retch |
| Dog is bright, eating, playful | May be more tired; cough is the main sign |
| No clear contagious contact | Often 3–10 days **after mixing with other dogs** |
| Usually settles quickly | Contagious; can last 1–3 weeks |

The tell-tale sign of **kennel cough** is that **honking, hacking cough that ends in a gag** — and a history of recent contact with other dogs at boarding, daycare, grooming, the park or a show.

## Why dogs get these infections

"Kennel cough" is really a group of viruses and bacteria (canine infectious respiratory disease complex) that spread easily wherever dogs gather. It's **highly contagious** but, in an otherwise healthy adult dog, often **mild and self-limiting**.

A **clear** nasal discharge with sneezing in a bright, happy dog is frequently just mild irritation or the early, minor phase of an infection.

## The warning signs that need a vet

Book a vet visit if you see any of these:

- **Thick, yellow, green or bloody** nasal discharge
- A cough that **lasts more than a week** or is getting worse
- **Poor appetite, lethargy, or fever**
- **Fast or laboured breathing** (this is urgent)
- Discharge from **one nostril only** (can signal a foreign body, tooth-root problem or growth)
- A **puppy, senior, or dog with other illnesses** — they're more vulnerable to pneumonia

## How it's treated

- **Mild cases:** rest, isolation from other dogs, and time — most clear in **1–3 weeks**.
- **Vet care:** cough relief, and **antibiotics** if a bacterial infection or pneumonia is suspected.
- **Supportive steps:** a **harness instead of a collar**, humid air (a steamy bathroom), and avoiding smoke and dust.

> A bright, eating, playful dog with a clear runny nose can often be monitored at home. A coughing, off-colour, or breathless dog — or any sick puppy or senior — should see a vet.

## Prevention

Keep your dog's **vaccinations up to date** (including the kennel cough vaccine if they board, mix or compete), keep a coughing dog **at home**, and clean shared bowls and toys.

## The bottom line

Dogs don't get human colds, but they do get contagious respiratory infections like kennel cough — recognisable by that honking, gagging cough after mixing with other dogs. Most mild cases pass with rest, but coloured discharge, a lasting cough, or any breathing trouble means it's time for the vet.
